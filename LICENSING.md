# License & commercial use

Barrelman is **source-available**. It is licensed under the **Apache License 2.0 with the
Commons Clause** — see [LICENSE](LICENSE) for the full legal text.

In one line:

> **You can use Barrelman for free — including inside your business — as long as you don't sell it.**

This page is a plain-language summary of the intent. It is **not** a substitute for the
[LICENSE](LICENSE), which is the binding legal agreement. If anything here conflicts with the
LICENSE, the LICENSE controls.

## What you can do

- **Self-host it for yourself, family, or friends.** Run Barrelman on your homelab or a personal
  server, for free, for any non-commercial use.
- **Run it inside your business.** Self-host Barrelman to power your own company's internal
  operations — for example, an on-prem GIS / geocoding API for your own apps and infrastructure.
- **Fork and modify it.** Change the code, experiment, and build on it.
- **Contribute back.** Fork it, improve it, and open a pull request.
- **Fork it and use it for your business**, as long as you don't sell the software or a service
  whose value is substantially just Barrelman (see below).

## What you can't do

The Commons Clause removes exactly one right from the Apache 2.0 license: the right to **Sell** the
software. "Sell" means charging a third party a fee for a product or service whose value comes
**entirely or substantially from Barrelman's functionality**. Concretely, you may not:

- **Host Barrelman as a paid online service or API** that others pay to use — especially one that
  competes with the official hosted version.
- **Sell a fork** of Barrelman, modified or not, as a paid product or hosted service.
- **Repackage or resell** Barrelman (or a close derivative) as your commercial product.

## Examples

**Allowed**

- You run Barrelman on your homelab for you and your friends.
- A company self-hosts Barrelman on its enterprise server to run an on-prem GIS API for its own
  internal business use — for free, on its own infrastructure.
- You fork Barrelman, add a feature, and submit it upstream.
- You fork Barrelman and use it to help run your own business, without selling the software itself.

**Not allowed**

- You self-host Barrelman and sell access to it as a paid hosted geocoding / search API, competing
  with the official hosted version.
- You fork Barrelman, rebrand it, and sell the modified version as a paid product or SaaS.
- Anything substantially equivalent to the above — reselling Barrelman's functionality to third
  parties for a fee.

## Commercial & enterprise licensing

Beyond free self-hosting, Barrelman is available under commercial terms — including a managed,
enterprise-grade GIS REST API, commercial licenses, dedicated support, and SLAs. (The Commons
Clause restricts *third parties* from selling the software; as the copyright holder, the project
can still offer it commercially.)

If you want a commercial or enterprise arrangement, or you'd like to offer Barrelman (or a
derivative) as a paid product or service, reach out to Alex Wohlbruck to discuss.
